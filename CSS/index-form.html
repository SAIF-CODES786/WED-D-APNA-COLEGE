Iron Man

Google Me Iron Man is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character was co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, developed by scripter Larry Lieber, and designed by artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby. The character made his first appearance in Tales of Suspense #39 (cover dated March 1963). He is a wealthy American business magnate, playboy, and ingenious scientist, Anthony Edward "Tony" Stark, who suffers a severe chest injury during a kidnapping. When his captors attempt to force him to build a weapon of mass destruction, he instead creates a mechanized suit of armor to save his life and escape captivity.

Publication History

Premiere

Iron Man premiered in Tales of Suspense #39 (cover dated March 1963), created by writer and editor Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, and artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby. The character was an immediate success, and received his own title in May 1968. The series ran for 332 issues before being renamed The Invincible Iron Man in 1978. The series ran until 1996, when it was canceled after issue #474. The character has been featured in several limited series, one-shots, and graphic novels, and has been a member of the superhero team the Avengers.

Fictional character biography

Anthony Edward "Tony" Stark is a wealthy American business magnate, playboy, and ingenious scientist. He suffers a severe chest injury during a kidnapping. When his captors attempt to force him to build a weapon of mass destruction, he instead creates a mechanized suit of armor to save his life and escape captivity. Later, Stark augments his suit with weapons and other technological devices he designed through his company, Stark Industries.
